# Sample Space

A **sample space** is the set of all possible outcomes of a random experiment.

It is usually denoted by $\Omega$.

### Example: Coin Flip

$$
\Omega = \{H, T\}
$$

### Example: Rolling a Die

$$
\Omega = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$

### Example: Two Coin Flips

$$
\Omega = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\}
$$

An **event** is a subset of the sample space.

Example:

$$
A = \{2,4,6\}
$$

is the event of rolling an even number from

$$
\Omega = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$
## Simple Sample Space

A **simple sample space** is a finite sample space in which all outcomes are equally likely.

If

$$
\Omega = \{\omega_1, \omega_2, \dots, \omega_n\}
$$

then

$$
P(\omega_i)=\frac1n
\quad \text{for all } i
$$

### Key Property

For any event $A$,

$$
P(A)=\frac{|A|}{|\Omega|}
$$

where:

- $|A|$ = number of outcomes in event $A$
- $|\Omega|$ = total number of outcomes in the sample space

### Example: Fair Die

$$
\Omega = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\}
$$

Let

$$
A = \{2,4,6\}
$$

Then

$$
P(A)=\frac36=\frac12
$$
