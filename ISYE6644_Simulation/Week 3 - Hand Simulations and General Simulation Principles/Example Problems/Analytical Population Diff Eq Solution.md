Aside: Solving analytically,

$$
f'(x)=2f(x)
$$

Separate variables:

$$
\frac{df}{dx}=2f
$$

$$
\frac{1}{f}\,df = 2\,dx
$$

Integrate both sides:

$$
\int \frac{1}{f}\,df = \int 2\,dx
$$

$$
\ln |f| = 2x + C
$$

Exponentiate:

$$
|f| = e^{2x+C}=e^C e^{2x}
$$

So

$$
f(x)=Ce^{2x}
$$

Use $f(0)=10$:

$$
10 = Ce^{0}
$$

$$
C=10
$$

Therefore,

$$
f(x)=10e^{2x}
$$
